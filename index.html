About AVCrossNet

AVCrossNet is an audio-visual speech enhancement (AVSE) network that leverages cross-modal attention between audio and lip-movement visual streams to improve speech quality and intelligibility in challenging noise conditions. Unlike audio-only approaches, AVCrossNet fuses complementary information from the speaker's lip movements with the noisy audio signal, enabling more robust enhancement—particularly at very low signal-to-noise ratios (SNR). The model is evaluated on the AVSEC-3 benchmark and compared against competitive audio-only and audio-visual baselines across a wide range of SNR conditions (−10 dB to +6 dB).

Each example below shows the silent lip-movement video (visual input to the model) alongside audio outputs from all compared systems. Listen across conditions to hear how AVCrossNet preserves speech clarity where baselines struggle.
